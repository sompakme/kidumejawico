    Pamela Anderson not wearing makeup is not really surprising. For a while now, the former model has repeatedly chosen to show up to event barefaced, and has been happier for it. Not wearing makeup has raised her self-esteem and made her feel more comfortable in her own skin, and now she wants her fans to feel the same.

    Pamela Anderson Shares Her Skincare Routine

    In the past few years, Pamela Anderson has decided to embrace her natural beauty and ditch makeup in favor of a barefaced look. That doesn't mean she doesn't appreciate the effort and talent that goes into wearing makeup, but she lately prefers to pay more attention to the products that will nurture her skin.

    She recently posted a video on her Instagram account where she shares her skincare routine in celebration of the first anniversary of Sonsie Skin, her own brand of skincare products. In the video, she's seen applying a Sonsie serum and a shiny lip gloss before running her hands through her hear, pushing it back, and flashing a smile.

    "@sonsieskin turns 1 year old today!" she wrote in the caption. "Our little family is getting bigger and bigger and I’m so grateful for all of you who have joined us… Together, we are spreading a message of self-care and self-love around the world."

    The model also hinted at new products that will come out in the next few months. "We have so many exciting things in the works this year and I can’t wait to share!" she said.

    "Be Sonsie... Be You!" she concluded. "With love, P."

    Pamela Anderson On Wearing Makeup

    Pamela Anderson surprised fans earlier this year by breaking her no-makeup streak for the Met Gala. Her makeup was understated, but still noticeable, which raised questions about whether she would be using makeup in public for now on. But if her social media posts are any indication, that doesn't seem to be the case, she probably just felt like it that night.

    Not using makeup was a conscious decision that Pamela Anderson made, but it wasn't, as she clarified, a "political statement." Instead, it was something that made her feel good about herself. "My name gives [even] me a false impression of myself," she explained in an interview with Allure.

    Being a model, she got too wrapped up in unattainable beauty standards that made her feel awful, and she needed to learn to love herself the way she is, without outside influences.

    "I’ve been on this journey, from Playboy to Baywatch — all these Halloween costumes, I like to say — and now come back home to self-love and self-acceptance, I just feel like it’s OK. [What I’m into now] is a conscious choice," she explained.

    "I’ve been trying and I’ve begun to feel closest to it... and it’s peeling back everything and trying to remember what my original thoughts are... It’s not just about makeup, taking off the mask, but it’s about why am I here, what’s my purpose, why do I resonate with people, can I help in any way?"

    That's what she's trying to do with Sonsie, most likely. Encourage women to be themselves and prioritize self-care over beauty standards.

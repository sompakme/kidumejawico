    Early Life

    Candice Bergen was born on May 9, 1946, in Beverly Hills, California. Her mother was a model, and her father was the famous actor, comedian, and ventriloquist, Edgar Bergen. She grew up in Beverly Hills and attended Harvard-Westlake School. At a young age, she made appearances on her father’s radio and TV programs, showcasing her early talent. Bergen attended the University of Pennsylvania but ultimately left before completing her degree. Despite this, she received an honorary doctorate from the university in 1992.

    Career

    After leaving college, Candice Bergen embarked on a successful career in acting, showcasing her versatility and talent in various film and TV projects. She started her journey in the industry as a fashion model, gracing the covers of esteemed magazines such as Vogue. Transitioning to the big screen, Bergen made her acting debut in the 1966 film The Group, followed by notable performances in movies like The Sand Pebbles (1966) and Carnal Knowledge (1971).

    Known for her ability to tackle both dramatic and comedic roles, Bergen’s performances have earned her critical acclaim and a dedicated fan base. However, it was her iconic role as Murphy Brown in the TV series of the same name that solidified her status as a beloved television actress. The success of Murphy Brown allowed Bergen to showcase her immense talent and comedic timing, earning her several awards and accolades throughout her career, including five Emmy Awards.

    Personal Life

    Candice Bergen has had an eventful personal life that has garnered much attention. She was married to French film director Louis Malle from 1980 until his untimely death in 1995. Together, they have a daughter named Chloé Malle who has also ventured into the film industry.

The couple has since enjoyed a long and stable marriage, weathering the ups and downs of life together. Their solid relationship is reflected in the multiple properties they own across the country, showcasing their financial stability and success.

    Throughout her life, Candice Bergen has also been involved in high-profile relationships. At one point, she dated music producer Terry Melcher, who had connections to the infamous Charles Manson. While her association with Manson and the Manson murders captured public attention, it is important to note that at the time of the horrific crimes, Bergen and Melcher had already moved out of the house that became the site of the tragic events.

    Despite the media scrutiny that comes with fame, Candice Bergen has managed to maintain a private personal life, focusing on her marriage, children, and overall well-being.

    Charles Manson Connection

    Candice Bergen’s connection to Charles Manson stems from her relationship with music producer Terry Melcher. Manson, an aspiring musician, believed that Melcher could help launch his career. In a notorious turn of events, Manson visited the house that Melcher and Bergen shared, which tragically later became the site of the Manson murders involving actress Sharon Tate.

    Despite speculations about Bergen’s involvement in the Manson murders, it has been clarified that Manson was aware that Bergen and Melcher were no longer residing in the house at the time of the gruesome crimes.

    Top 10 Rich Entrepreneurs Of 2022

    Here is the list of top 10 rich entrepreneurs in 2022, We have collected the data from various authentic sources on the internet and made calculations accordingly. Elon Musk is the worlds richest entrepreneur, with a net worth of $219 billion as of May 25, 2022.

    25 Aug 2024, 00:00

    Here is the list of top 10 rich entrepreneurs in 2022, We have collected the data from various authentic sources on the internet and made calculations accordingly.

    1. Elon Musk, $198.7B Real-Time Net Worth 

    Elon Musk is the world’s richest entrepreneur, with a net worth of $219 billion as of May 25, 2022.

    Elon Musk’s strategy of taking over Twitter in April 2022 resulted in a purchase of $44 billion.

    He intends to put $21 billion of his own money into the purchase. Musk sold 9.6 million Tesla shares worth $8.5 billion in the weeks leading up to the takeover announcement.

    2. Jeff Bezos, $129.8B Real-Time Net Worth

    Jeff Bezos is the world’s second-richest entrepreneur, with a net worth of $171 billion.

    According to Amazon’s proxy statement for 2022, Jeff owns 10% of the Amazon company he founded in 1994.

    Jeff Bezos launched the e-commerce behemoth Amazon in his Seattle garage. In July 2021, he stood down as CEO to become executive chairman.

    3. Bernard Arnault & Family, $140.9B Real-Time Net Worth

    the Bernard Arnault Family is the world’s third-wealthiest family, with a net worth of $158 billion.

    The 73-year-old French billionaire is the owner of 70 of the world’s most recognizable fashion and cosmetics companies, including Louis Vuitton, Tiffany & Co., and Sephora.

    In January 2021, LVMH paid $15.8 billion for American jeweler Tiffany & Co, reportedly the most significant luxury brand acquisition ever.

    Arnault is married to concert pianist Helene Mercier and lives in Paris, France.

    4. Bill gates, $125.2B Real-Time Net Worth

    Bill Gates is the fourth richest entrepreneur in the world, with a net worth of $129 billion.

    Gates is thought to have donated $35.8 billion in Microsoft stock to the Foundation, making it the World Health Organization’s most significant private donor.

    Bill and Melinda announced their divorce on Twitter after 27 years of marriage in May 2021. 

    They are still co-chairs of the Gates Foundation. When Gates stepped down from the Microsoft board of directors in March 2020, he owned roughly 1% of the company’s stock. 

    Until now, Bill Gates has given the Gates Foundation an estimated $35.8 billion in Microsoft stock.

    5. Warren Buffet, $111.7B Real-Time Net Worth

    Warren Buffet is the fifth richest entrepreneur in the world, with a net worth of $118 billion.

    Warren Buffett, known as the “Oracle of Omaha,” is one of the world’s most successful investors.

    He is the CEO of Berkshire Hathaway, which owns Dairy Queen.

    He has pledged to contribute 99 percent of his fortune. He has donated around $45 billion so far, mainly to the Gates Foundation and his children’s foundations. 

    Unlike many of the other billionaires on this list, Buffet still lives in the same house in Omaha, Nebraska, that he purchased for $31,50 in 1958 before becoming wealthy.

    6. Larry Page, $91.7B Real-Time Net Worth

    Larry Page’s net worth has increased from a little under $52 billion in March 2020 to $111 billion as of 2022. 

    In December 2019, Larry Page stepped down as CEO of Alphabet, the parent company of Google, while he remains a board member and a controlling shareholder.

    Like several other software millionaires on our list, Larry Page began his journey to fame and money in a college dorm room. 

    Page and his friend Sergey Brin came up with the idea of better internet data extraction while at Stanford University in 1995.

    Page was an early investor in worldary Resources, a business dedicated to space exploration and asteroid mining.

    He’s also invested in Kitty Hawk and Opener, two businesses that make “flying cars.”

    Google’s stock rose about 50% in 2021, propelling Page and Brin to the top of the billionaire list. 

    7. Sergey Brin, $88.3B Real-Time Net Worth 

    Sergey Brin has a net worth of $107 billion as of May 2022. 

    The Russian-American business magnate and computer scientist is presently the world’s seventh wealthiest entrepreneur. 

    Sergey’s net worth as a co-founder of Google and previous president of Alphabet should be no surprise to anyone. His earnings come primarily from being one of Alphabet’s key owners.

    8. Larry Ellison, $94.1B Real-Time Net Worth 

    He is the eighth richest entrepreneur in the world, with a net worth of $106 billion. 

    Larry Ellison is the chairman, chief technology officer, and co-founder of Oracle, which he controls around 35% of.

    Oracle is most commonly used in CRM databases. 

    It also took a huge step forward in cloud computing with its recent acquisition of NetSuite. 

    In 2020, Ellison permanently shifted to the Hawaiian island of Lanai, which he purchased nearly whole for $300 million in 2012.

    9. Steve Ballmer, $85.9B Real-Time Net worth

    According to Forbes, his personal worth was roughly $91.4 billion in April 2022, making him the ninth-richest entrepreneur in the world. 

    Steve Ballmer was Microsoft’s high-wattage former CEO from 2000 to 2014.

    Steven Anthony Ballmer is a business entrepreneur and investor from the United States. He is the current owner of the National Basketball Association’s Los Angeles Clippers (NBA).

    He has boosted his philanthropy since 2014, donating roughly $2 billion to a donor-advised fund dedicated to lifting Americans out of poverty.

    10. Mukesh Ambani, $98B Real-Time Net Worth 

    Mukesh Ambani is the tenth richest entrepreneur in the world, with a net worth of $90.7 million.

    However, there may be a discrepancy because Mukesh Ambani’s net worth is $95.6 billion, according to Bloomberg Billionaires Index sources.

    Reliance Industries, the world’s largest oil refining facility, is controlled by Ambani. Other activities of the Mumbai-based group include a nationwide 4G wireless network.

    The billionaire also owns a $400 million home residence and the Mumbai Indians professional cricket team.

    Hugh Bonneville Biography

    Hugh Bonneville (full name: Hugh Richard Bonneville Williams) is the best-recognized British actor to feature as Robert Crawley, Earl of Grantham in the Downton Abbey historical drama of ITV, a role that won him two consecutive nominations for the Emmy Award alongside a Golden Globe nomination. He is also well known for Mr Brown in the Paddington film.

    Born to a nurse and urological surgeon, he grew up in a family with no links to the world of entertainment. Bonneville grew up with a dream to become a lawyer. As a young man, though, he changed his mind.

    After studying at Dulwich College Preparatory School and Sherborne School, he went to study acting at the London Webber Douglas Academy of Dramatic Art. His professional career began with theatrical plays and eventually moved to television in 1990. Bonneville also started to act simultaneously in films and has since performed consistently on both the small and the big screens. The actor born in London is extremely benevolent and often gives to charities.

    Hugh Bonneville Downton Abbey

    He is best known for playing Robert Crawley, Grantham’s Earl in Downton Abbey’s ITV historical drama series. This is a British historical television series set in the early 20th century, created by Julian Fellowes. The series first aired on ITV in the United Kingdom on September 26, 2010, and on PBS in the United States, which supported series production as part of its Masterpiece Classic anthology, on January 9, 2011.

    Hugh Bonneville Notting Hill

    His early roles were usually good-natured bumbling characters such as Bernie in Notting Hill (1999). Notting Hill is a romantic comedy film directed by Roger Michell in 1999. The screenplay was written by Richard Curtis, author of Four Weddings and a Funeral (1994), and was produced by Duncan Kenworthy. The movie stars Julia Roberts, Hugh Grant, Rhys Ifans, Emma Chambers, Tim McInnerny, Gina McKee, and Hugh Bonneville.

    Hugh Bonneville began his acting theatre career, making his first appearance at the Open Air Theatre. He became part of the National Theater in 1987 and appeared in numerous plays. He made his television debut with the drama series ‘ Chancer’ in 1990. A year later, the actor joined the Royal Shakespeare Company where he performed in’ Hamlet’ production.

    He landed a film role in the horror drama film ‘ Mary Shelley’s Frankenstein’ in 1994. Two years later, Bonneville starred at the short-lived sitcom’ Married for Life.’ He had roles in the television series’ Get Well Soon’ and’ Mosley’ in the years that followed. He finished the decade with an appearance in the romantic comedy-drama flick ‘ Mansfield Park.’

    Jennifer Aniston’s net worth is estimated to be over $320 million. With her uber-successful acting career and various business ventures, she’s clearly not hurting for money. But the actress stands to grow her personal fortune amid rumors she’s considering writing a memoir – and it could make her up to $20 million richer.

    Recently, a report Aniston is weighing the pros and cons of writing a book about her life. She’s apparently inspired by her late friend Matthew Perry, who released a controversial memoir a few years before his 2023 death.

    Aniston has been married twice before, first to Brad Pitt and Justin Theroux. Though her marriage to Pitt ended nearly two decades ago, many questions still linger about what happened, especially given the widely held belief he cheated with his co-star Angelina Jolie while filming Mr. and Mrs. Smith. They debuted their romance shortly after his divorce news from Aniston was publicized, then went on to marry and have six kids together.

    “Jen knows people want to hear all about her love affair with Brad and the truth about their divorce, especially since Brad and Angelina have now split and Jen and Brad have reconnected,” a source told Life & Style.

    “Jen has had a lot of therapy to make peace with her failed marriage to Brad and other romances that didn’t work out,” they went on. “But there is so much more to the story, especially about why they never had a child together.”

    There’s been intense speculation about Aniston’s fertility over the years, including if having different opinions on kids is what drove her and Brad apart, considering he went on to have six kids of his own (and reportedly wants more with his current girlfriend Ines De Ramon).

    But a few years ago, Jennifer admitted there was once a time where she wanted a child and was actively trying. The actress also emphasized how hurtful the speculation about her family plans were.

    While Aniston hasn’t commented on the claims she’s considering a memoir, the price tag that could accompany a book is likely tempting. Prince Harry reportedly earned a $20 million advance for his memoir, released last year, and stood to make more from sales. Likewise, Britney Spears were estimated to have made at least $12.5 million for her memoir The Woman in Me. Britney has already expressed a desire to write a second book, so it seems like a lucrative venture for celebs, one that Aniston may soon take advantage of.

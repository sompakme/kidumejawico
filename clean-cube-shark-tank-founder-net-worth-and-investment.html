    The Founder of Clean Cube

    Clean Cube, the innovative food storage company, was founded in 2017 by the dynamic duo, Jake Sendowski and Michelle Strausman. This California-based company was born from Michelle’s personal experience and need for an efficient way to store leftover chicken stock. The Sendowskis, along with their business partner, have nurtured Clean Cube from its humble beginnings to the thriving family-owned business it is today. Notably, Lori Greiner, a prominent Shark Tank investor, is a stakeholder in the company with a 5% share.

    Jake Sendowski, one of the co-founders, brings a wealth of knowledge to Clean Cube with his master’s degree in electrical engineering from the esteemed California Institute of Technology. His technical expertise and innovative mindset have played a crucial role in the development and success of the company. Michelle Strausman, the other co-founder, is an educator who graduated from UCLA. Her practical approach and keen eye for consumer needs have contributed significantly to the product’s design and functionality.

    The Product and Pricing

    Clean Cube is a convenient food storage device designed to simplify the portioning and storage of soups, broths, and leftovers. Made of food-grade silicone material, the Clean Cube is a large ice tray that allows you to freeze and store your favorite foods in convenient portions. With its flexible construction, you can easily remove frozen cubes for reheating or use them directly in cooking.

    The Clean Cube product line includes the Souper Cubes tray, available in various sizes to suit different portion needs. From 2 tablespoons to 2 cups, there is a tray size for every requirement. The original design is a single-serving cup in an aqua hue, which adds a touch of style to your freezer.

    Souper Cubes on Shark Tank

    Souper Cubes, the innovative food storage solution, made a significant impact when it appeared on Shark Tank in Season 12 Episode 14. Co-founders Jake Sendowski and Michelle Strausman presented their product to the Sharks, seeking a $400,000 investment in exchange for a 5% equity stake in their $8 million company.

    During their pitch, Sendowski and Strausman showcased the versatility and convenience of Souper Cubes, demonstrating how it simplifies portioning and storage of soups, broths, and leftovers. Their goal was to secure a deal that would help them further grow their business and expand their reach.

    The episode generated tremendous excitement and interest, leading to a surge in sales for Souper Cubes. In just 24 hours following the airing of the episode, the company recorded an astounding $938,000 in revenue.

    “Appearing on Shark Tank was a game-changer for us. The exposure and validation from the Sharks gave us the boost we needed to take Souper Cubes to the next level. The response from customers has been overwhelming, and we are grateful for this incredible opportunity.”

    After receiving multiple offers from the Sharks, Sendowski and Strausman carefully considered their options. Ultimately, they accepted a deal from renowned entrepreneur and Shark, Lori Greiner. Greiner invested $400,000 in Souper Cubes for a 5% equity stake in the company.

    The partnership with Greiner not only brought experienced guidance but also provided valuable exposure to a wide audience. With her extensive reach and influence, Greiner’s investment solidified Souper Cubes’ position in the food storage industry.

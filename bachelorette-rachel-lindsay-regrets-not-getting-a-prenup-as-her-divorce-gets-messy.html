    Fans are curious to know what happened between Bachelorette alum Rachel Lindsay and her husband Bryan Abasolo. While it remains unclear exactly why the couple are divorcing, it’s clear the reality star has regrets. Right now, the thing that’s top of mind for her is that they didn’t sign a prenup before getting hitched.

    “Rachel is kicking herself for not having a prenup,” a source claimed to Life & Style. “She wanted one, but Bryan pushed the idea away and she let it go.”

    Rachel’s Ex Could Win Spousal Support

    Close

    Though Bryan has a chiropractic business, it’s apparently not doing well financially, and he’s considering spousal support from Rachel, which he could win, considering she’s worth more. “He’s going after her for spousal support — and he just might get it,” the source said.

    Even if her estranged husband doesn’t win big in their divorce, it could cost Rachel a lot in legal fees, the insider warned. “She’s worth a few million,” they claimed. “She’s hired a top notch lawyer, but this could wind up costing her plenty.”

    Rachel herself has confessed that she wishes she got a prenup, admitting it would’ve made her current situation less complicated (and threatening to her net worth).

    “The reason I didn’t [get a prenup] is because the place I’m in now financially … is totally different than when I got married,” she admitted during an appearance on the “Hidden Gems With Natasha Parker” podcast earlier this month. “We were more level and I wasn’t living in California.”

    Reflecting on their marriage that took place in 2019, Rachel described it as a "different time" in her life. Back then, she was working as a lawyer and didn’t have the same professional and financial opportunities that she enjoys today, largely thanks to her involvement in the Bachelor franchise.

    Many ‘Bachelor’ Break-Ups Get Nasty Over Money

    These factors influenced Rachel’s decision not to pursue financial protection through a prenuptial agreement, something she clearly looks back on with regret. Rachel and Bryan’s divorce remains ongoing.

    In the tumultuous world of The Bachelor and Bachelorette franchise, some break-ups have made headlines for their messy aftermaths, particularly when significant financial implications were at stake, and it appears Rachel and Bryan can be added to the list.
